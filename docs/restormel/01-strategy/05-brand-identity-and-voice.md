# Restormel Brand Identity and Voice

## Purpose
This document defines the brand identity, positioning, voice, and design direction for Restormel as the developer platform brand under Allotment Technology.

Restormel is the public-facing platform brand for developer tools that make graph-native AI systems visible, understandable, and configurable.

SOPHIA remains the reference application and public demonstration of the platform.

---

## Brand role in the ecosystem

### Parent company
**Allotment Technology Ltd**

Role:
- company and legal entity
- strategic umbrella
- long-term ecosystem owner

### Platform brand
**Restormel**

Role:
- developer platform brand
- package namespace
- product family identity
- public-facing infrastructure story

### Showcase application
**SOPHIA**

Role:
- public demo
- flagship reference app
- proof of capability
- narrative showcase for the platform

---

## Brand idea

**Restormel helps people see what their AI systems are doing.**

The brand should feel:
- technically credible
- visually intelligent
- calm rather than noisy
- exploratory rather than corporate
- powerful without feeling opaque

This is not a generic AI brand.
It should not feel like a neon prompt wrapper.
It should feel like a thoughtful, graph-native observability and reasoning platform.

---

## Positioning statement

**Restormel is the developer platform for graph-native AI systems, combining visual debugging, graph-aware retrieval, structured reasoning, and provider-flexible tooling in one self-serve environment.**

Shorter version:

**Restormel helps developers visualize, debug, and ship graph-native AI systems.**

---

## Brand pillars

### 1. Visibility
Restormel makes hidden system behavior visible.

### 2. Composability
Restormel products are modular and usable in many products, not just SOPHIA.

### 3. Graph-native intelligence
Restormel is built around structure, relationships, paths, and grounding, not just prompts.

### 4. Calm technical confidence
The brand should feel rigorous and modern without hype.

### 5. Accessibility across skill levels
It must work for senior technical professionals and for capable novice builders using AI-assisted coding.

---

## Brand personality

Restormel should feel like:
- an intelligent systems guide
- a mapmaker for complex AI workflows
- a calm analyst rather than a loud salesman
- opinionated about clarity
- precise without being cold

### Personality keywords
- lucid
- observant
- structured
- technical
- exploratory
- trustworthy
- modern
- quietly confident

### Avoid
- breathless hype
- cyberpunk cliches
- generic SaaS jargon
- forced playfulness
- vague claims about “revolutionizing AI”

---

## Voice and tone

### Core voice
Restormel speaks like a technically fluent product team that understands complex systems and explains them clearly.

### Tone attributes
- clear
- measured
- practical
- quietly ambitious
- intelligent
- unpretentious

### Writing principles
- prefer plain language over inflated language
- show what the system does before making claims
- use examples early
- explain complexity through structure
- speak to builders as capable people
- avoid unnecessary abstraction

### Good examples
- “See the path that produced the answer.”
- “Graph-aware retrieval for AI apps.”
- “Paste a trace. Inspect the graph. Find the weak link.”
- “Structured reasoning as an API.”

### Avoid examples
- “Transform your enterprise with next-generation AI innovation.”
- “Unlock the future of cognition.”
- “Revolutionary intelligence infrastructure.”

---

## Audience-aware tone

### For technical developers
Use direct, specific language.
Example:
- “Import a trace, inspect the graph, and highlight the answer path.”

### For vibe coders and novice builders
Use simpler entry language without sounding patronizing.
Example:
- “Upload your AI trace and see what happened.”

### For technical buyers
Use operational clarity.
Example:
- “Deploy as a hosted SaaS, use your own provider keys, and control model access by project.”

## Visual identity direction

The sloth image suggests a useful visual tone: observant, calm, understated, slightly dry. That can work if used sparingly as a supporting brand character, not the whole brand.

The main design language should feel like:
- graph maps
- layered signal
- topology and paths
- dark analytical interfaces
- calm editorial structure

### Visual motifs
- nodes and paths
- graph contours
- layered panels
- dotted density fields
- directional traces
- expandable clusters
- inspector drawers
- contrast between overview and detail

### UI character
- dark-first product aesthetic
- warm neutral foundation with precise accent colors
- minimal chrome
- clean panel edges
- soft rounding
- visible hierarchy
- generous whitespace
- analytical rather than decorative motion

## Suggested color system

This is a starting point, not a locked brand system.

### Core neutrals
- **Ink Black**: `#0B0D12`
- **Charcoal**: `#141821`
- **Slate**: `#1E2430`
- **Steel**: `#8A94A6`
- **Mist**: `#D9E0EA`
- **Paper**: `#F5F7FA`

### Primary brand accent
- **Signal Teal**: `#2EC4B6`

### Secondary accents
- **Path Blue**: `#4C8DFF`
- **Amber Insight**: `#FFB84D`
- **Coral Alert**: `#FF6B6B`
- **Violet Depth**: `#7C6CFF`

### Semantic mapping
- claims / core nodes: Path Blue
- evidence / sources: Signal Teal
- warnings / contradictions: Coral Alert
- synthesis / summary: Amber Insight
- advanced / hidden / inferred: Violet Depth

This palette supports graph visualisation and product UI without feeling overdesigned.

## Typography

Use one strong, modern sans and one optional mono.

### Primary sans
Preferred options:
- **Inter**
- **Söhne** if licensed
- **IBM Plex Sans** if you want something more technical-editorial

Recommended default:
**Inter**

Why:
- highly legible
- modern but neutral
- works across marketing and product UI
- easy for implementation

### Mono
Preferred options:
- **IBM Plex Mono**
- **JetBrains Mono**

Recommended default:
**IBM Plex Mono**

Use for:
- code snippets
- graph metadata
- traces
- API examples

## Layout and component style

### Layout principles
- grid-based
- large clear headings
- obvious product segmentation
- strong card system
- layered panels for docs and console
- side inspector patterns

### Component feel
- rounded but not bubbly
- soft shadowing or subtle borders
- dark surfaces with clear separation
- restrained motion
- micro-animations for graph expansion, path highlighting, and trace playback

---

## Logo direction

Restormel does not need a literal graph logo.
It needs a mark that suggests:
- structure
- connection
- routes
- observability

Potential directions:
1. abstract node-path mark
2. topographic contour / route mark
3. simplified cartographic symbol
4. a subtle stylized “R” with connected joints

The sloth can remain a secondary mascot or cultural asset, but should not be the main identity for enterprise-facing platform materials unless intentionally stylized.

## Brand do / do not

### Do
- make the invisible visible
- be specific about capability
- show the product in action
- write with calm clarity
- favor technical credibility over hype

### Do not
- overclaim intelligence
- sound mystical
- lean on generic “AI for everyone” language
- overuse mascot energy
- make visuals noisy or chaotic
