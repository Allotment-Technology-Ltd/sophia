## Core rules encoded in prompts

- Answer the specific question asked; do not drift into generic exposition.
- Establish a clear conclusion/angle early and build the case toward it.
- Keep structure explicit: strong opening, argumentative middle, concise ending.
- Use paragraph discipline: each paragraph should make one relevant point, support it, and show relevance.
- Engage counterarguments directly and fairly.
- Prefer precise, clear prose over rhetorical flourish.
- Use only relevant evidence and avoid filler.
- Conclude by drawing threads together without repeating the whole argument.
- Keep writing suitable for high-quality academic essays: logically signposted and exam-ready.
