## The Core Idea
Explanatory power, simplicity, and fit with evidence jointly guide rational preference. Inference to the best explanation (IBE) does not deliver certainty; it ranks competing hypotheses by explanatory virtues.

This is a bridge lesson between basic logic and advanced essay writing: you learn to justify why one explanation is rationally preferable while acknowledging uncertainty.

## Why This Matters
Many philosophical and social arguments compare rival explanations of complex phenomena. Strong writing does not claim "proved forever." It argues:
- this explanation has wider scope,
- fewer ad hoc assumptions,
- better predictive coherence.

Then it states residual uncertainty honestly.

## Worked Comparison
Case: rising classroom disengagement.

Hypothesis A:
Mainly caused by declining student motivation.

Hypothesis B:
Mainly caused by assessment design and attention-fragmenting platform conditions.

To compare, ask:
1. Which explains more of the evidence?
2. Which requires fewer unsupported assumptions?
3. Which predicts new data better?

A better essay can prefer B while admitting mixed causation and limits.

## Common Mistake
Equating best explanation with final proof.

IBE supports provisional preference, not logical necessity.

## Before You Write (Step 2)
Pick one social phenomenon and compare two hypotheses in this order:
1. scope,
2. simplicity,
3. predictive fit,
4. cautious conclusion.
