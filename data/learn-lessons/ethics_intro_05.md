## The Core Idea
Dialectical quality rises when critique targets the strongest version of a claim. Steelmanning means reconstructing an opposing argument so that its best logic is visible, even if you still reject it.

This is a decisive upgrade from debate posture to philosophical method. Weak objections produce weak learning. Strong objections force conceptual clarity.

## Why This Matters
If you only attack strawmen, your conclusion may feel safe but remains untested. Degree-level writing expects you to engage with serious alternatives and show why your position still holds.

Steelmanning improves three skills at once:
1. fairness,
2. analytical precision,
3. synthesis quality.

## Worked Example
Weak rebuttal:
"People who support surveillance just do not care about freedom."

Steelmanned opponent:
"Targeted surveillance may be justified when evidence thresholds and judicial oversight minimize abuse while preventing severe harm."

Now your critique can engage real tradeoffs: accountability, rights, proportionality, and empirical effectiveness.

## Common Mistake
Confusing agreement with steelmanning.

You can present an opposing view in its strongest form without adopting it.

## Before You Write (Step 2)
Build your paragraph in this sequence:
1. "Best version of opposing claim..."
2. "Strongest reason for it..."
3. "My response..."
4. "What survives from the opposing insight..."
